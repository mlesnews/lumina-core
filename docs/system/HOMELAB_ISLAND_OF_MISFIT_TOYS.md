## The Philosophy

### Why Misfits Matter

1. **They represent effort** - Someone started them for a reason
2. **They contain lessons** - Each unfinished project teaches something
3. **They might be ahead of their time** - Today's misfit = tomorrow's feature
4. **They're part of the story** - LUMINA's autobiography includes failures

### The Homelab Promise

> *"No project is truly abandoned. It's just waiting on the island until we're ready."*

The homelab doesn't judge. It doesn't delete. It holds space for:
- Ideas that seemed good but didn't work out
- Features that got interrupted
- Experiments that need more time
- Dreams that are still forming

### Paradigm Shifts

The three literary references are all about **paradigm shifts**:

1. **Lord of the Flies**: The paradigm of civilization collapses
2. **Animal Farm**: The paradigm of equality corrupts into tyranny
3. **Who Moved My Cheese?**: The paradigm of stability is disrupted

**Applied to Homelab:**
- Projects exist in a paradigm (current tech, current priorities)
- When paradigms shift, some projects get left behind
- Without `#COULDASHOULDA`, they fall into the Upside Down
- The rescue mission is a paradigm-aware recovery

### The Warning

> *"The Upside Down is not a punishment. It's what happens by default."*

Every unrecovered misfit toy doesn't go to a nice shelf - it falls into the Upside Down where:
- Code rots (dependencies go stale)
- Context is lost (why was this built?)
- Lessons evaporate (the @FAFO never happens)
